# Competitive Analysis: Fathom

## Overview

**Company**: Fathom
**Website**: https://fathom.ai
**Category**: AI Meeting Notetaker
**Positioning**: "Never take notes again" - Free-forever AI notetaker for individuals and teams

## Their Key Products

| Product | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Fathom Notetaker** | Core product - joins meetings, transcribes, summarizes |
| **Ask Fathom** | Search across all meetings with natural language |
| **Team Edition** | Collaborative features, CRM sync, team analytics |

## Their Strengths

1. **Free Forever Model**: Individual plan genuinely free with unlimited recording/storage
2. **Simplicity**: Clean, distraction-free interface with minimal learning curve
3. **Accuracy**: 90-95% transcription accuracy, 5.0/5 rating on G2 (4,600+ reviews)
4. **25 Language Support**: Broad international coverage
5. **CRM Integrations**: Native sync with Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Notion, Asana
6. **Instant Summaries**: Delivered straight to inbox after each call

## Their Weaknesses

1. **Visible Bot**: Joins as "Fathom Notetaker" participant - no stealth mode
2. **No Mobile App**: Cannot initiate recordings on mobile (browser-only access)
3. **Bot Disruption**: Some users report pop-ups and bot presence interrupts natural flow
4. **Limited Analysis**: Focused on note-taking, not deep research analysis
5. **No Voice-First**: Traditional meeting recorder, not conversational setup

---

## How We Beat Them

### Strategy 1: Voice-First Research (Different Category)

**Our Advantage**: Fathom records meetings; we conduct and analyze research conversations.

**Tactics**:
- Position as "research platform" not "meeting notetaker"
- Emphasize structured analysis (lenses, themes, evidence) vs. simple summaries
- Our AI asks questions; theirs just listens

**Feature Comparison**:
| Feature | Fathom | UpSight |
|---------|--------|---------|
| AI asks questions | No | Yes (LiveKit agent) |
| Research frameworks | No | Yes (Lenses) |
| Evidence extraction | No | Yes |
| Theme clustering | No | Yes |

### Strategy 2: No Bot Required

**Our Advantage**: We don't need to join meetings as a visible participant.

**Tactics**:
- "Upload recordings without awkward bots"
- Emphasize post-meeting analysis for sensitive research
- Support for any audio/video source, not just live meetings

### Strategy 3: Mobile-First Voice

**Our Advantage**: Voice capture and conversation on any device.

**Tactics**:
- "Capture research insights from your phone"
- Voice orb works anywhere
- No desktop requirement

## UX Patterns to Learn From

### What They Do Right

1. **Free-Forever Positioning**: Removes friction, builds trust
2. **Inbox Delivery**: Summaries arrive where users already are
3. **One-Click Timestamps**: Jump to any moment instantly
4. **Clean Dashboard**: Distraction-free, recordings front and center
5. **"Ask Fathom" Search**: Natural language across all meetings

### What We Should Adopt

- **Inbox/Slack Delivery**: Push insights to where teams work
- **Timestamp Navigation**: One-click jump to moments
- **Search-First Architecture**: Natural language queries across all data

## Threat Assessment

**Threat Level**: Low-Medium

**Why**: Different category (notetaker vs. research platform). Could become a threat if they add research analysis features, but their DNA is broad meeting productivity, not focused research.

**Watch For**:
- Addition of research-specific features
- Integration with research tools
- Movement into user research market
