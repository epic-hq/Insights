## Their Key Products

| Product | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **AI-Moderated Interviews** | Autonomous AI conducts voice/video/text interviews with adaptive follow-ups |
| **Participant Recruitment** | Built-in access to millions of pre-qualified participants across demographics and geographies |
| **Multi-Modal Analysis** | Captures facial expressions, vocal tone, and text responses |
| **Stimulus Testing** | Test videos, images, landing pages, Figma prototypes mid-interview |
| **Fraud Detection** | Proprietary system reducing invalid responses from ~20% to near 0% |
| **Reporting** | Auto-generated reports, highlight reels, presentations, personas, themes |
| **Multi-Language** | 50+ languages with automatic translation |

## Their Strengths

1. **Massive Scale**: Thousands of simultaneous AI interviews — no human bottleneck
2. **Speed**: Full study from recruit → interviews → insights in <24 hours (vs 4+ weeks traditional)
3. **Enterprise Logos**: Microsoft, Google, Nestlé create enormous credibility
4. **Sequoia Backing**: $69M at $500M valuation means serious runway and visibility
5. **Recruitment Network**: Built-in participant panels eliminate the "find people to talk to" problem
6. **Fraud Detection**: Genuine differentiator — bad data is the #1 complaint about scale research
7. **Multi-Modal Capture**: Facial expressions + vocal tone + text gives richer signal than text-only
8. **Viral GTM**: Memorable billboard hiring stunt drove press coverage

## Their Weaknesses

1. **AI-Only Moderation**: No human-in-the-loop option for sensitive or complex topics
2. **Per-Study Model**: No longitudinal intelligence — each study is a snapshot, no cumulative knowledge
3. **No Repository Layer**: Results are reports, not a queryable evidence base
4. **Enterprise-Focused**: Pricing and sales motion geared toward large orgs, not SMBs/solos
5. **No Synthesis Across Studies**: Can't connect findings from study A to study B over time
6. **No Evidence Traceability**: Reports summarize but don't let you click-through to source moments
7. **No Action Integration**: Insights → report. Not insights → tasks → outcomes
8. **Trust Gap for B2B**: "AI talked to your stakeholders" is a hard sell for consulting engagements
9. **Generic Analysis**: No research lenses or structured frameworks for extraction

### Why We Don't Compete Head-On

**Different buyer, different job, different budget.**

Listen Labs solves: "We need to run 500 interviews but don't have enough researchers."
UpSight solves: "We already talk to customers—but insights scatter and decisions ride on opinions."

| Dimension | Listen Labs | UpSight |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Buyer** | Research/insights team at enterprise | Founders, consultants, PMs at small teams |
| **Core job** | Scale research without researchers | Turn conversations into defensible decisions |
| **Intelligence model** | Snapshot per study | Cumulative over time |
| **Value moment** | "Here's what 500 people said" | "Here's what's changed across 6 months of conversations" |

### Our Key Advantages

1. **Longitudinal Intelligence**: Every conversation compounds knowledge. They start fresh each study.
2. **Evidence Traceability**: Click theme → see exact quote + who said it + context. They generate reports.
3. **Human + AI Hybrid**: Users lead when rapport matters. AI assists. Consultants can say "I talked to your team."
4. **Cross-Source Synthesis**: Calls + notes + tickets + surveys in one view. Not just interviews.
5. **Accessible Price Point**: $99/mo vs enterprise pricing. Different market entirely.

## Strategic Response

### Do NOT

- Build a Listen Labs clone — can't outscale a $500M Sequoia-backed company
- Position against them directly — different ICP, invites unfavorable comparison
- Chase enterprise research teams — that's their turf

### DO

1. **Sharpen "Human + AI Hybrid" positioning** — pure AI has a trust gap for consulting and B2B
2. **Own "Longitudinal Intelligence"** — our cumulative knowledge model is structurally impossible for per-study tools
3. **Double down on evidence traceability** — "Anyone can summarize. Almost no one can prove."
4. **Evolve the Insights page** into a synthesis + action dashboard (see bead Insights-XXXX)
5. **Test shipped surveys first** — validate engagement before building AI moderation
6. **Plan lightweight AI interviews** for later — human designs guide, AI conducts, human reviews

### Sequence

1. **Now**: Validate shipped survey feature, improve Insights page
2. **Next**: Cross-conversation synthesis + stakeholder perspectives view
3. **Later**: Lightweight AI async interviews (hybrid model)

## Threat Assessment

**Threat Level**: MEDIUM (upgraded from LOW if they move downmarket)

**Why Medium**:
- Not in our buyer conversations today
- Different ICP and price point
- But: $500M valuation means they'll chase growth, SMB tier likely
- Market education effect: "AI interviews" becoming expected baseline

**Watch For**:
- Self-serve tier launch (downmarket move)
- Repository/longitudinal features (encroaching on our moat)
- Acquisition by research platform (Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey, Dovetail)
- Pricing transparency (signals market maturation)
