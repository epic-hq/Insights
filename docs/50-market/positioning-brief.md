> **Turn customer conversations into defensible decisions—fast.**

## The Big Problem

**Teams talk to customers constantly. Decisions still ride on opinions.**

Every week, your team runs discovery calls, support tickets, sales conversations, and user interviews. The conversations happen. The insights don't stick.

**What actually happens:**
- Notes live in 5 different tools (Notion, Slack, Zoom, email, someone's head)
- AI summaries are ungrounded text—no receipts, no trust
- "We heard from customers that..." becomes a political weapon
- Same discovery gets repeated because no one can find what was already learned
- Decisions get made on whoever speaks loudest, not what customers actually said

**The cost:**
- Product builds the wrong thing (again)
- Sales loses deals they should've won
- CS misses churn signals until it's too late
- Leadership debates "what customers want" instead of acting on evidence

## Who This Is For (ICP)

### Primary: Small Teams Who Talk to Customers

| Segment | Profile | Why They Buy |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Startup founders (5-50 people)** | Wearing multiple hats, running discovery themselves | Need to move fast without losing signal |
| **Solo/boutique consultants** | Running stakeholder interviews, producing deliverables | Hours saved = money earned |
| **Product/UX leads at SMBs** | No dedicated research team, doing it themselves | Need a system, not another tool |
| **Fractional executives** | Interim roles, need to ramp fast | Prove recommendations with evidence |

### Common Traits

- **Talk to customers weekly** (not quarterly research projects)
- **Distributed or async** (can't rely on hallway conversations)
- **Resource-constrained** (no budget for Dovetail + Gong + dedicated researcher)
- **Decision-makers or close to them** (insights need to drive action, not reports)

### Who This Is NOT For

- Enterprise teams with dedicated research ops (they have Dovetail)
- Sales-only orgs who just want call coaching (they have Gong)
- Teams who don't talk to customers (we can't help you)

### The Core Value Props

**1. Grounded, Not Generated**
Every insight links back to who said it, when, and in what context. Click any theme → see the exact quotes. No more "AI said so."

**2. One Place, Not Five Tools**
Calls, notes, tickets, surveys—all synthesized together. Stop hunting across Notion, Slack, and someone's memory.

**3. Decisions, Not Decks**
Insights roll into tasks with owners and deadlines. Evidence stays attached. Research doesn't die in a slide deck.

**4. Human + AI, Not AI-Only**
AI assists; humans lead. Choose voice agent, guided interview, or just upload recordings. Your workflow, not ours.

## Battle-Testing Checklist

### Does this positioning pass?

- [ ] **Specific enough?** Can someone say "that's not me" and be right?
- [ ] **Differentiated?** Would a competitor struggle to claim the same thing?
- [ ] **Provable?** Can we demonstrate this in a demo or trial?
- [ ] **Valuable?** Does the ICP care about this problem?
- [ ] **Believable?** Can a small team credibly deliver this?

### Red Flags to Watch

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| "Sounds like Dovetail" | Differentiation not landing |
| "We don't do research" | ICP framing too narrow |
| "What's the AI do exactly?" | Value prop unclear |
| "Seems expensive for what it is" | ROI not articulated |
| "We'd need to change our workflow" | Positioning as replacement, not enhancement |
